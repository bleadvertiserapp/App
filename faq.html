What is BLE Advertiser?

+

BLE Advertiser is an advanced Android application that turns your phone into a Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) peripheral simulator. You can create custom BLE advertisement profiles, broadcast them, and test BLE scanners and IoT firmware ‚Äî all without any extra hardware.

What are the minimum requirements?

+

BLE Advertiser requires:

  • Android 8.0 (Oreo) or higher
  • A device with BLE peripheral support
  • Bluetooth and Location permissions (required by Android for BLE)

For extended advertising (251 bytes), your device must support BLE 5.0.

How do I create my first BLE profile?

+

Tap the "+" button ‚Üí Choose a predefined template (iBeacon, Eddystone, Heart Rate, etc.) or create a fully custom profile ‚Üí Configure your advertisement data, services, and settings ‚Üí Save ‚Üí Toggle the switch to start advertising.

Does BLE Advertiser work on all Android phones?

+

BLE Advertiser works on most modern Android devices. However, not all devices support BLE peripheral mode (advertising). The app will check your device's capabilities at startup and let you know which features are available. Extended advertising (BLE 5.0) requires newer hardware.

Is an internet connection required?

+

No. BLE Advertiser works completely offline. All your profiles, configurations, and logs are stored locally on your device. An internet connection is only needed to download the app from Google Play and validate subscriptions.

What is the difference between Legacy and Extended Advertising?

+

Legacy Advertising: Uses 31-byte packets compatible with all BLE devices. Supported on all Android phones with BLE peripheral mode.

Extended Advertising: Uses up to 251-byte packets, available only on devices that support BLE 5.0. Allows you to include more data such as longer service data, multiple UUIDs, and rich manufacturer data.

What automation triggers are available?

+

BLE Advertiser supports 8 automation triggers:

  • Manual: Toggle advertising on and off manually
  • Duration: Advertise for a set time period
  • Schedule: Set start/end times with recurring days
  • Location (Geofence): Start/stop based on entering or leaving a geographic area
  • WiFi: Trigger based on connected WiFi network
  • Charging/Battery: Start when charging or below a battery threshold
  • Motion: Start based on device movement detection
  • Boot: Auto-start advertising when the phone reboots

Can I run multiple profiles at the same time?

+

Yes! You can run multiple BLE advertising profiles simultaneously. Each profile can have its own configuration, advertisement data, GATT services, and triggers. The number of concurrent advertisements depends on your device's BLE hardware capabilities.

What predefined profile templates are available?

+

BLE Advertiser includes templates for:

  • iBeacon (Apple's proximity beacon format)
  • Eddystone (Google's beacon format ‚Äî UID, URL, TLM)
  • AltBeacon (open-source beacon standard)
  • Heart Rate Monitor
  • Blood Pressure Cuff
  • Thermometer
  • Custom Profile (full control over all fields)

üîß Troubleshooting

Why isn't my advertisement showing up on BLE scanners?

+

Check the following:

  • Bluetooth is turned ON
  • Location permission is granted (Android requirement for BLE)
  • Location services are enabled on your device
  • The profile toggle is ON
  • Your advertisement packet doesn't exceed the size limit (31 bytes for legacy)
  • Your scanner is close enough and scanning for the correct service UUIDs

Advertising stops when I close the app. How do I fix this?

+

To keep advertising running in the background:

  • Ensure you have Pro subscription (background advertising is a premium feature)
  • Disable battery optimization for BLE Advertiser in your phone's settings
  • Don't swipe away the persistent notification
  • On some manufacturers (Xiaomi, Samsung, Huawei), you may need to allow the app to "auto-start" in their custom battery settings

My device doesn't support extended advertising. Why?

+

Extended advertising requires BLE 5.0 hardware support. While your phone may advertise BLE 5.0, the actual Bluetooth chipset may not support all BLE 5.0 features. This is a hardware limitation and cannot be resolved via software. You can check your device's BLE capabilities on the app's Device Info screen.
